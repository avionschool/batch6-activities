Think slower, learn faster!

I know this may sound like a paradox and confusing but let me explain this. The reason why I love this quote is that it hits me right in the gut. When I was younger, I always do things fast and think fast without thinking too much into the problem and without understanding what it teaches me. I just want to do it fast so that I can feel that I have done something, but that is not the case. When I did that, I didn't understand the process it teaches and it only got me confused even more. When I first heard about this or read this, at first I didn't practice it, but when I did, I learned and understood faster than before. Thinking slow is being in the moment of understanding what the problem is and thinking of what it actually means. With that, you will learn more about the problem by thinking deeply into it. So think slow, and learn fast!
